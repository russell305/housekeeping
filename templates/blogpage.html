Price, Service, Location.

4/22/2019

To get a house cleaner in Miami their are many options. Search Engines offer many individual websites, some better than others. Craigslist offers cleaners who decide to post their listing that day and pay $5. Many listings only give partial info, requiring you to make several calls before finding the person you need. On this platform we are able to compare prices, services, and location of house cleaners.

PRICING:

The price for a cleaning of a 1/1 can vary from $65 to over $120. Other Cleaners only charge by the hour, in three hour sets. Some people may be price sensitive and require a cleaning at the low price range. ( Most likely not as thorough.) Here we provide a way where users can quickly compare prices from different providers without having to call several, write down and compare.

SERVICES:

Some cleaners bring all necessary cleaning supplies, some bring partial supplies, and some bring nothing and you provide. Once, I hired a house cleaner for $90 on Groupon for a 1/1. I assumed he would bring a broom and mop because I have ceramic floors. Upon arrival he informed me that he just vacuums the tile floors with his vacuum. I still had to mop after paying him to clean! Here we have a comprehensive checklist where house cleaners can check which supplies they bring.

LOCATION:

Closer is always better. Miami traffic is ridiculous and only getting worse. To live in Aventura and have a house cleaner come from Kendall can take over 90 minutes of travel each way. Costing time, gas, delaying start time, etc. Wouldn’t it be nice to have someone who can clean your home right around the corner?

What is a Cuban Mop?

4/27/2019

A Cuban Mop is one of the simplest, most efficient tools used to clean a floor. It is a piece of wood in the shape of a T with an absorbent cloth folded on bottom T part. The folded cloth provides a large surface area for quick cleaning. When the cloth gets really soiled, you can just rinse it out and refold on bottom of mop. When your done cleaning you can wash the cloth and you don’t have a dirty wet mop hanging around.

I believe the Cuban Mop was the inspiration for the wildly successful Swiffer Sweepers. Notice how they have very similar designs. The difference is the Swiffer is designed to have you purchase their chemical cleaning towels draining your wallet in perpetuity. Also when your finished these dirty towels will be one more item which will end up in the ocean. I don’t think I’ve ever had a cleaning service in Miami come over with a Swifter Sweeper. If they did I would be wary.

The Cuban Mop is originated in the Caribbean and was brought to Miami. It is called “el palo de trapear” which translates to “the mopping stick”. Again, simple and efficient. Here are the simple steps to create your own cuban mop.

  • Immerse towel in cleaning solution.
  • Squeeze and fold towel around T stick
  • Push the towel around the room to clean the dirt.
  • When cloth gets soiled, remove.
  • Rinse and repeat.

    • The Cuban Mop will clean your floors faster, cheaper, and more eco-friendly than other mopping choices. Make sure you or your housecleaner are using one!
